Tracking success

Using data from Kane County, we were able to track the outcomes of 224 cases that went through the 16th Circuit Treatment Alternative Court.

Few are accepted

Of the applications, 81% were rejected, highlighting the relatively limited circumstances where a mental health court is used.

It doesn't always work

Of those accepted, 59% successfully completed the program. Half of those who failed the program were sent to a facility run by the state prison system.

Other examples

In Tazewell County, 38% of people who have gone through mental health court have graduated, but the overall structure of the program remained the same.

Systemic Patterns

The contrast suggests that geography plays a decisive role in program outcomes across the state.
