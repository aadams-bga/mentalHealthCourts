Tracking success

Using data from Kane County, we were able to track the outcomes of cases that went through the 16th Circuit Treatment Alternative Court.

Few are accepted

Of the applications, 81% were rejected, highlighting the relatively limited circumstances where a mental health court is used.

Long-Term Commitment

Because the program takes years, many participants remain Still Active in the system today.

Concluded Cases

Of those who reached a conclusion, 59% graduated. Half of those who didn't graduate were sent to state prison.

Regional Variation

In Tazewell County, 38% of concluded cases graduated, but the overall structure remains similar across judicial circuits.

Consistent Patterns

Neighboring Peoria County reported a higher graduation rate of 68% for participants who completed the program.

Statewide Similarities

While local data varies, the systemic challenges of access and outcome consistency persist across all 102 counties.
